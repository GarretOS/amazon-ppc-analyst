## Amazon PPC Analyst
**TNT Ecommerce Inc. | May 2024 to Aug 2025 | Calgary, Canada (Remote)**

TNT Ecommerce was a boutique agency specializing in CPG and food brands. Fewer accounts. Deeper focus. No large team. Just the work, the data, and the results.

I managed two accounts that tested completely different mandates.

**Canadian Food Brand**
The account came in at $23K monthly with TACoS already at a healthy 11%. The strategy was straightforward: keep expanding, keep the data clean, don't break what's working. By July 2025, revenue had grown to $95K monthly. TACoS hit as low as 7.5% at peak efficiency.

**Canadian Health & Nutrition Brand**
This one started at $68K monthly and 42.1% TACoS. Month one, the client wanted aggressive sales growth. I pushed the campaigns hard. Revenue climbed. Then the mandate shifted to maximum profitability. I rebuilt the entire bidding strategy in one week. By mid-2025, revenue had nearly doubled to $126K+. TACoS came down from 42% to 15.8%.

Two completely different goals. Both delivered.

Part of my role also included co-authoring business intelligence presentations to pitch and close new clients, including an established Canadian food brand with strong retail presence.

## Amazon PPC Manager & Team Lead
**Seller Interactive | Aug 2021 to Sep 2022 | Markham, Canada (Remote)**

I joined Seller Interactive with less than a year of PPC experience. Within months, I was promoted to Team Lead.

At peak, I directly managed 12 to 15 PPC specialists. I oversaw 100+ accounts with $800K+ in monthly ad spend across a 50+ person department.

The agency role wasn't just about managing campaigns. It was about building the structure that let everyone else manage theirs.

I developed a department-wide campaign creation and bid optimization system and rolled it out across the PPC team. I trained the Account Management team so they could have real, data-backed conversations with clients about their account performance.

One day a client threatened to leave. The account manager couldn't resolve it. I took the call. Instead of defending the PPC numbers, I asked them to share their listing. I went through everything: the title, the bullet points, the A+ content. The problem wasn't the ads. It was a listing conversion issue.

The client didn't just stay. They ended the call ready to keep working with us.

PPC doesn't exist in isolation. It's one part of a system. If you only look at the ads, you'll miss where the real problem lives. That lesson is in everything I do now.
